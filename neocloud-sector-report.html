What's Driving the Collapse

  • Supply catch-up: NVIDIA shipped aggressively; H100s are no longer scarce
  • Hyperscaler price wars: AWS slashed H100 pricing by 44% in mid-2025
  • Next-gen displacement: Blackwell offers 4-5x faster inference — NVIDIA CEO joked you "couldn't give Hoppers away"
  • Custom ASIC competition: Google TPU, AWS Trainium, Microsoft Maia all offering cheaper AI compute
  • Spot market pressure: Vast.ai/RunPod driving race-to-bottom on spot pricing

Implications for Neoclouds

  • Collateral erosion: GPU-collateralized loans face shrinking collateral values as rental rates and resale values drop
  • Margin compression: Revenue per GPU falling while debt service costs are fixed
  • Depreciation manipulation: CoreWeave extended GPU depreciation periods — inflating EBITDA but not changing economics
  • Fleet replacement cost: First-gen infrastructure (2021-22 wave) hitting depreciation limits, forcing upgrades
  • The era of rewarding companies simply for amassing GPUs is definitively over

Neocloud vs. Hyperscaler: The Competitive Dynamic

The cost and speed advantages that birthed the neocloud sector are narrowing as hyperscalers invest in AI-native infrastructure and custom silicon.

Neocloud Advantages

  • Cost: 66% cheaper than hyperscalers — H100 at $34/hr vs $98/hr on hyperscaler (Uptime Institute, 2024)
  • Speed: Deploy in days/weeks vs months on hyperscalers; lean teams, agile infra
  • Specialization: Purpose-built for AI training/inference — no legacy overhead
  • InfiniBand networking: Superior interconnect for large-scale distributed training
  • Kubernetes-native: CoreWeave's K8s stack optimized for GPU workloads
  • No lock-in: Less ecosystem stickiness enables customer flexibility
  • Sustainability: Crusoe offers 100% renewable energy — increasingly matters for enterprise procurement

Hyperscaler Counter-Attack

  • Custom silicon: AWS Trainium, Google TPU, Microsoft Maia eliminate NVIDIA dependency
  • Price cuts: AWS cut H100 pricing 44% — can afford to bleed on GPU margin
  • Ecosystem depth: Developer tools, security, compliance, managed services
  • Balance sheet: $100B+ cash reserves vs neocloud debt-fueled expansion
  • Supply securing: Ramped Blackwell procurement, reducing the "access gap"
  • AI platforms: Bedrock (AWS), Vertex AI (GCP), Azure AI Studio create full-stack lock-in
  • Irony: CoreWeave's top customers (Microsoft, Meta, OpenAI) are also building their own capacity

The Closing Window Key Risk

The gap that allowed neoclouds to thrive — hyperscaler GPU scarcity and long lead times — is closing. Hyperscalers have ramped deployment timelines, secured forward Blackwell supply, and begun re-optimizing stacks for AI-native developers. The question is no longer whether neoclouds can win on access, but whether they can win on platform value. Companies that don't invest in software risk being relegated to GPU arbitrage — the lowest-margin layer of the AI stack.

The GPU Debt Spiral Structural Risk

CoreWeave pioneered GPU-collateralized debt — borrowing against GPUs to buy more GPUs. The DDTL loans are secured by hardware whose rental rates have already fallen 50-70%, shrinking collateral value just as repayment schedules begin. The business model requires perfect execution: borrow billions, buy GPUs, build data centers fast, generate revenue immediately to service debt. Any delay in that chain breaks the machine. Short-seller Jim Chanos has highlighted extended depreciation periods as accounting manipulation. Wall Street now prices a 40% chance of default within 5 years.

Sector Outlook & Conclusion

Three Scenarios for the Neocloud Sector

Bull Case (30% probability): The New Cloud Tier

AI demand continues accelerating. CoreWeave refinances successfully, reaches profitability by 2027. 3-5 neoclouds establish themselves as a permanent tier between hyperscalers and bare-metal. Sector reaches $180B+ by 2030. CRWV trades above $150.

Base Case (45% probability): Consolidation & Survival

AI demand moderates from peak growth rates. CoreWeave refinances but at punitive rates, compressing margins. Weaker neoclouds (Paperspace already gone) are acquired or shut down. 2-3 survivors (CoreWeave, Lambda, Crusoe) carve out a niche but hyperscalers recapture share through custom silicon and price cuts. Sector reaches $80-120B by 2030. CRWV trades $60-100.

Bear Case (25% probability): The GPU Debt Crisis

AI demand disappoints or enterprise ROI remains unproven. CoreWeave fails to refinance $4.2B at viable rates, triggers restructuring. GPU collateral values crater as Blackwell floods market. Cascading defaults across neocloud debt instruments. NVIDIA absorbs losses on CoreWeave backstop. Surviving workloads migrate to hyperscalers. The neocloud category is remembered as the "CDO of AI" — debt instruments backed by rapidly depreciating silicon.

The Bottom Line

The neocloud sector is the purest leveraged bet on AI infrastructure demand. The growth is real — 200%+ per year — but the business model is structurally fragile: debt-funded, hardware-centric, dependent on a single supplier (NVIDIA), serving a small number of customers who are actively building alternatives. CoreWeave is the sector's bellwether and its greatest risk. If it refinances successfully and maintains customer commitments, the sector has a path to permanence. If it doesn't, the contagion could redefine how the market prices AI infrastructure risk. For investors: this is the AI trade's most asymmetric subsector — massive upside if the cycle continues, massive downside if it doesn't. Size positions accordingly.
