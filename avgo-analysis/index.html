Thesis Driver

Broadcom is not an AI infrastructure bet — it IS the AI infrastructure. Its custom ASIC franchises are locked in 3–5 year co-design cycles with the world's largest AI spenders, making it the only company capable of delivering $100B in AI chip revenue by FY2027.

With ~70% custom ASIC market share, 6 confirmed hyperscaler customers (Google, Meta, Apple, OpenAI, ByteDance, unnamed 5th), and a $73B AI order backlog representing 18 months of forward revenue visibility, Broadcom has structurally detached from the commodity chip cycle. VMware's software segment adds a recurring $27B+ revenue base at 93% gross margin. Hock Tan's track record of disciplined M&A and consistent beat-and-raise execution is among the best in semiconductors.

Competitive Moat Deep

  • SerDes/networking IP: Broadcom's Tomahawk and Jericho switch chips are the de-facto standard in hyperscale data centers — customers buy the whole stack
  • Co-design depth: Broadcom engineers are embedded in Google, Meta, and Apple chip design teams — cannot be replicated quickly
  • Scale advantage: At $40B+ AI revenue, R&D amortization per customer is vastly superior to any challenger
  • TSMC partnership: Priority access to N3/N2 process nodes for AI ASIC production

vs. NVIDIA: Coexistence, Not Competition Context

  • NVIDIA dominates training: H100/B200/B300 GPUs for large model training runs
  • Broadcom serves inference: Custom ASICs optimized for specific model architectures at lower power/cost per inference token
  • Hyperscaler math: At scale, custom silicon is 2–5x more efficient than general-purpose GPUs for inference workloads
  • The threat: NVIDIA's NVLink Fusion + Marvell ($2B investment, Apr 1 2026) attempts to enter ASIC integration — a real but 2–3yr risk

Risk Framework

Risks are quantifiable and mostly known. Key monitoring signals: Google TPU insourcing signals, NVIDIA/Marvell NVLink Fusion product timelines, and AI CapEx guidance from hyperscalers.

Google Insourcing Risk

Probability: ~10%Severity: HIGH
Google designs its own TPU architecture but relies on Broadcom for implementation IP and networking. Full insourcing would remove 35–50% of AI semi revenue. Timing: 3–5 years minimum if decision made today.

NVIDIA / Marvell Ecosystem Threat

Probability: ~20%Severity: MODERATE
NVIDIA's $2B investment in Marvell (Apr 1, 2026) for NVLink Fusion integration is the most credible new competitive threat. Creates alternative custom silicon ecosystem. 2–3yr runway before impact.

AI CapEx Cycle Deceleration

Probability: ~15%Severity: HIGH
If hyperscaler CapEx guidance reverses (macro downturn, regulatory action, AI commercialization disappointment), Broadcom's $100B target becomes unachievable. $73B backlog provides 18-month buffer.

Apple WiFi Insourcing

Probability: ~60% (happening)Severity: LOW
Apple is already insourcing WiFi chips — approximately 4.3% of total AVGO revenue at risk. Apple's new custom AI chip win partially offsets this. Net revenue impact likely <2% over 2 years.

$100B FY2027 Target Miss

Probability: ~25%Severity: MODERATE
Missing the $100B target by 20–30% would cause multiple compression but not an existential event. Even $70B in AI revenue at 68% EBITDA margins produces a highly valuable company at current valuation.

ByteDance / Export Control

Probability: ~30%Severity: MODERATE
ByteDance is a named AI ASIC customer. Any escalation in China export restrictions could immediately halt shipments. Revenue exposure undisclosed but flagged as material by management.

Mandatory Bear Case — The Other Side of the Trade

  • 58x trailing PE is expensive — even for a compounder. The market is pricing significant perfection into the FY2027 narrative.
  • Extreme customer concentration: Google alone is estimated at 35–50% of AI semi revenue. A single design win loss or insourcing decision would be catastrophic to near-term estimates.
  • VMware customer pushback: Aggressive VCF migration pricing has generated documented enterprise complaints. Software growth decelerated to +1% QoQ in Q1 FY2026 — watch for further deceleration.
  • Software momentum risk: At 87% VCF conversion, the pool of remaining upsell is shrinking. Software segment growth will naturally decelerate toward 5–8% by FY2027 as conversion completes.
  • $64B debt constrains optionality: Broadcom cannot pursue major M&A for the next 2–3 years while paying down VMware debt. The next secular shift could require capital it does not have flexibility for.
  • NVIDIA Marvell investment (Apr 1, 2026): NVLink Fusion is NVIDIA's explicit attempt to build a competing ecosystem for custom silicon integration — existential if it gains traction with hyperscalers.
  • $100B target credibility window: Hock Tan's FY2027 $100B target requires roughly 2.5x revenue growth in 2 years. Even with backlog visibility, execution risk is real and any quarterly miss will compress the multiple sharply.

Management & Execution Track Record

Hock Tan is among the most credible semiconductor CEOs of the past two decades. His M&A playbook and operational discipline are a core component of the investment thesis.

Hock Tan — CEO Track Record HIGH Credibility

  • CEO since 2006 — grew from ~$2B to $64B+ in annual revenue
  • Executed transformative acquisitions: LSI Logic (2014), Avago/Broadcom merger (2016), CA Technologies (2018), Symantec Enterprise (2019), VMware (2023)
  • Consistent beat-and-raise: 14 consecutive dividend increases, multi-year earnings guidance reliability
  • VMware acquisition initially met with deep skepticism (paid 12x revenue); now executing at 78% operating margin — widely viewed as masterstroke
  • Playbook: acquire → cut non-core → raise prices → expand margins → generate cash → repeat

Investment Recommendation

BUY — High Conviction | Base Target: $460 | Bull: $550

At ~$300 per share, Broadcom offers exceptional risk/reward for investors with an 18–24 month time horizon. The combination of (1) structural AI ASIC dominance with $73B in visible backlog, (2) 6-customer diversification actively improving, (3) a near-monopoly software cash flow engine at 93% gross margin, and (4) Hock Tan's execution track record creates a compelling asymmetric setup.

The core bear case — Google insourcing — is real but represents a 3–5 year execution risk, not an imminent catalyst. At 30x forward earnings, the market is already pricing in meaningful execution discount vs. the $100B target. The $73B backlog provides hard downside support. Position sizing should reflect the customer concentration risk; this is not a set-and-forget holding.
